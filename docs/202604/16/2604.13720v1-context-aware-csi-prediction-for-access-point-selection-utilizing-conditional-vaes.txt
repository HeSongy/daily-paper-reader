# Context-Aware CSI Prediction for Access Point Selection Utilizing Conditional VAEs 

Franz Weißer, Amar Kasibovic, and Wolfgang Utschick 

TUM School of Computation, Information and Technology, Technical University of Munich, Germany 

{franz.weisser, amar.kasibovic, utschick }@tum.de 

Abstract —Indoor wireless communication environments are strongly influenced by dynamic conditions, which affect channel state information (CSI) and, consequently, the precoding strategy and the selection of the access point (AP). Device-free sensing and localization functionalities can provide information about these conditions, including, for example, the user’s position and the position of mobile blocking objects. To model the statistical relationship between the CSI and the provided conditions, we employ a conditional variational autoencoder (cVAE). We treat the user and object positions — referred to as context information — as conditional inputs to the cVAE. The proposed model does not rely on ground-truth CSI and is trained directly on noisy data. Once trained, the framework can infer channel statistics solely from user and blocking object positions, enabling proactive AP selection based on inferred statistical CSI without requiring continuous CSI estimation. Extensive simulations with the state-of-the-art ray-tracing tool Sionna validate the proposed method. 

Index Terms —Conditional variational autoencoder, machine learning, access point selection, position-based precoding 

I. I NTRODUCTION 

Future systems will utilize a higher density of access points (APs) to meet the needs of an increasing number of con-nected mobile user equipments (UEs) while remaining energy-efficient. In such dynamic communication systems, knowledge of the channel state information (CSI) is crucial for achieving a reliable connection and, consequently, high data rates. More specifically, the multiple possible APs need information about their communication link to the served UE to design and update their transmit strategy. The CSI is highly dependent on current radio propagation characteristics and the UE’s position, making the interplay between communication and sensing functionalities a core enabler for such dense systems. Typically, based on the CSI and other context information, the UE is connected to one of the APs, cf. [1], [2]. The UE’s position may be provided by radar, lidar, or joint communication and sensing (JCAS) functionalities, or, if the deployment is outdoor, by global navigation satellite system (GNSS). The JCAS functionalities also support device-free sensing of moving obstacles, which may significantly influence the current CSI but are not part of the communication system. In time-varying scenarios, i.e., when the UE and/or obstacles are moving, the CSI at the APs must be updated regularly to ensure an optimal connection for the UE. Furthermore,

anticipating the optimal serving AP ahead of time is crucial for ensuring continuous service to the UE and meeting the quality of service demands of the communication system. Hence, many works have been dedicated to predicting such 

handovers based on a set of contextual information to prepare the APs in advance and ensure a reliable connection, e.g., [1], [2]. Such context information can include, but is not limited to, the UE’s position, speed, and the position of any dynamic blocking obstacles. Additionally, position-based radio maps have been devel-oped, e.g., in [3], [4]. Here, the path loss and, consequently, the signal-to-noise ratio (SNR) for each UE position and AP are estimated, which enables SNR-based AP selection. Furthermore, position-based precoding strategies for each re-spective AP have been introduced, e.g., [5]–[7]. However, in dynamic scenarios characterized by continuously changing environments, these methods fail to adapt to the current radio propagation characteristics. Hence, we propose a new method that adapts to such dynamic scenarios while providing statisti-cal CSI to perform localization- and sensing-aware precoding and AP selection. 

Contributions: Building on the recent idea of position-based precoding from [7], which uses a multi-view generative model, we develop a framework based on the conditional variational autoencoder (cVAE) that provides channel statistics solely from the UE’s position and other context information. To this end, a cVAE is trained on noisy training samples to infer conditional channel statistics. These learned channel statistics are not only used to select the optimal AP but also to provide linear precoders for downlink transmission. We empirically demonstrate the benefit of learning the conditional channel statistics from all available context information. II. P RELIMINARIES

Each block increases the number of convolutional channels. Lastly, a linear layer maps to the respective outputs in the latent space. For the decoder input, we append the context information to the latent realization z, increasing the overall decoder’s input size. The remaining decoder network mirrors the encoder network’s structure as in [12]. The last layer is a linear layer that maps to the decoder network’s outputs. In contrast to the original work in [10], the prior network does not share any structure or weights with the encoder. We implement the prior network using two linear layers, with a hidden dimension that is 8 times the latent space dimension, and include a batch normalization layer and the ReLU activation function after the first linear layer. IV. S IMULATIONS 

A. Baseline Methods 

We compare the proposed statistical CSI prediction tech-nique with the following baseline methods. In [7], a coupled Gaussian mixture model (CGMM) is con-structed to infer channel statistics based on the user’s position. We extend this by including the whole context information c as input to the CGMM. Additionally, we compare to a geometric approach that simply chooses the closest, unblocked AP and transmits solely over the line-of-sight (LoS) path, as proposed in [5], [6]. We denote this approach by Geo-LoS . We also compare to two variants of the cVAE. The first variant neglects the blocking position b and solely uses the user position r as context information ( cVAE-pos ). The second variant uses the latent space representation obtained from the encoder network, based on ˜y and c, which requires additional knowledge of every CSI for evaluation ( cVAE-csi ).

cVAE-context , to the baseline techniques in terms of AP selection accuracy and the normalized rate. For the latter, we normalize the rate obtained with a given CSI prediction and AP selection technique by the rate achieved with perfect CSI knowledge. For the training, we simulate the channel responses at each AP for L = 1 .8 · 10 5 different user and blocking positions uniformly distributed across the room using the indoor model introduced in Section II-B. Lastly, we observe that the conditional Gaussian assumption for the context information itself, as used in the CGMM, yields inferior results across all test sets. V. C ONCLUSION 

This work demonstrates how context-aware CSI prediction and AP selection can be achieved using a cVAE. The proposed technique is used to predict statistical CSI solely based on the user’s position and the position of a moving obstacle. The results across three representative test cases demonstrate the overall benefit of learning radio propagation characteristics and exploiting all the available contextual information.
