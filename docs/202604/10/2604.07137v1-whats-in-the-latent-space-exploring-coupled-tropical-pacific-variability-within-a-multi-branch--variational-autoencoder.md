## 摘要
在耦合的热带太平洋气候场上训练的多分支 $β$-变分自编码器的潜在空间中编码了什么？为回答这一问题，我们将模型训练于热带太平洋的海表温度（SST）、海洋热含量（OHC）和向外长波辐射（OLR），使用长达500年的工业前控制模拟，并评估其重建能力和物理可解释性。结果表明，该模型具有良好的泛化性能，从训练到测试的性能仅有轻微下降，同时保留了三个气候场在海盆尺度上的主要结构。潜在空间诊断显示，变化在各维度上的分布并不均匀：海表温度主要集中在较少的潜在维度中，而海洋热含量和向外长波辐射则分布更广，涉及多个维度。与传统的热带太平洋诊断结果比较发现，部分潜在维度与已知的厄尔尼诺和拉尼娜变率相对应，而其他维度则捕捉到年代际及更长时间尺度上的耦合海气变率。灵敏度实验和潜在空间遍历进一步识别了与类东太平洋型、类中太平洋型、沿岸型、次表层主导型和大气主导型变率相关的维度。总体而言，这些结果表明，多分支 $β$-变分自编码器能够提供一个有技能且具有物理意义的耦合热带太平洋变率的降维表示。

## Abstract
What is encoded in the latent space of a multi-branch $β$-variational autoencoder trained on coupled tropical Pacific climate fields? To answer this question, we train the model on sea surface temperature, ocean heat content, and outgoing longwave radiation across the tropical Pacific, using a 500-year preindustrial control simulation, and evaluate both reconstruction skill and physical interpretability. The model generalizes well, with only modest degradation from training to test performance, and preserves the dominant basin-scale structure of all three fields. Latent-space diagnostics show that variability is organized unevenly across dimensions: sea surface temperature is concentrated in a smaller subset of latent dimensions, whereas ocean heat content and outgoing longwave radiation are more broadly distributed across multiple dimensions. Comparisons with conventional tropical Pacific diagnostics further show that several latent dimensions align with known El Niño and La Niña variability, while others capture related coupled ocean-atmosphere variability on decadal or longer timescales. Sensitivity experiments and latent traversals identify dimensions associated with eastern-Pacific-like, central-Pacific-like, coastal, subsurface-dominant, and atmosphere-dominant variability. Together, these results show that the multi-branch $β$-variational autoencoder yields a skillful and physically informative reduced representation of coupled tropical Pacific variability.
